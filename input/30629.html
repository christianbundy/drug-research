Early Comparison of Celexa and Lexapro
Escitalopram (Lexapro) & Citalopram (Celexa)
by Ady
Citation:   Ady. "Early Comparison of Celexa and Lexapro: experience with Escitalopram (Lexapro) & Citalopram (Celexa) (ID 30629)". Erowid.org. Apr 21, 2010. erowid.org/exp/30629

 
DOSE: 40 mg oral Pharms - Citalopram (daily)
  10 mg oral Pharms - Escitalopram (daily)

BODY WEIGHT: 75 kg


After a period of approximately five months taking celexa for social and generalised anxiety disorder, I started to notice that the effects, particularly those concerned with tackling my anxiety disorders gradually started to taper away. Thus making me feel more anxious and self-concious.

My doctor told me this effect is known to occur after a long bout of treatment with celexa, so naturally put me on lexapro which has a similar side effect profile and appears to be of particular benefit to people with anxiety disorders. Because the transition to escitalopram from citalopram was instant, I could feel a noticeable improvement in my symptons by the fourth day (of what is currently a 1 weeks use). I found that my anxiety levels, particularly those concerning my social and general anxiety improved significantly to somewhat about two thirds of what they were before.

The anti-depressant effect seemed to be more prominent on lexapro, in that the 'so what, who cares' mentality seemed to be more noticeable and prominent-when put in comparison to celexa.

I noticed that the side effect profile of celexa and lexapro was pretty similar:- a feeling of wanting to yawn and stretch and a slight distinct 'drugged up' feeling that persists for about a couple of hours. But on the whole, the side effects in my case were easily bearable.

Overall, I feel that lexapro seems to be working well (takes about 3-4 weeks for the full apparent anxiolytic, anti-depressant effect), but it remains to be seen my depression and related anxieties are reduced to an acceptable level.
